## 📰 Summary of the Threat
The Flipper Zero is a portable, multi-tool device designed for hacking and penetration testing. The recent announcement that Flipper Devices will continue firmware development with a smaller internal team and greater reliance on community contributions may introduce new security risks. As the development process becomes more open, there is a potential for malicious actors to contribute vulnerable or malicious code to the firmware. This could lead to a compromised device that can be used for malicious purposes, such as unauthorized access to systems or data breaches.

The primary targets of this potential threat are individuals and organizations using the Flipper Zero device for legitimate purposes, such as penetration testing or security research. If a malicious actor contributes vulnerable code to the firmware, it could be exploited by other attackers, putting the device's users at risk. The technical mechanism of this vulnerability is related to the open-source development process, where community contributions are not always thoroughly vetted. This can lead to the introduction of backdoors, privilege escalation vulnerabilities, or other security flaws.

The impact of this threat is significant, as a compromised Flipper Zero device could be used to gain unauthorized access to sensitive systems or data. Additionally, the device's multi-tool capabilities make it an attractive target for malicious actors, who could use it to conduct a wide range of attacks, from wireless hacking to RFID exploitation. As the development process becomes more open, it is essential for users and contributors to be aware of the potential risks and take steps to mitigate them.
