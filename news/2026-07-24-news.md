# 🚨 Clop ransomware targets Windchill, FlexPLM in data theft attacks
**Source**: BleepingComputer
**Link**: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/clop-ransomware-targets-windchill-flexplm-in-data-theft-attacks/
**Date**: 2026-07-24

## 📰 Summary of the Threat
The Clop ransomware gang is targeting internet-exposed instances of PTC Windchill and FlexPLM in a new data theft extortion campaign. Windchill and FlexPLM are product lifecycle management (PLM) and product data management (PDM) software solutions used by various industries, including aerospace, automotive, and manufacturing. The Clop ransomware gang is exploiting vulnerabilities in these systems to gain unauthorized access, steal sensitive data, and then demand a ransom in exchange for the stolen data. The gang is known for its double-extortion tactics, where they not only encrypt the victim's data but also threaten to release the stolen data publicly if the ransom is not paid.

The technical mechanism of the vulnerability or attack involves the exploitation of internet-exposed Windchill and FlexPLM instances that have not been properly secured or patched. This allows the attackers to gain access to the system and steal sensitive data, including product designs, engineering documents, and other confidential information. The attackers are likely using a combination of reconnaissance, vulnerability scanning, and exploitation techniques to identify and compromise vulnerable systems. The use of internet-exposed systems without proper security controls in place provides an easy entry point for the attackers, highlighting the importance of securing and monitoring these systems.

The impact of this threat is significant, as it can result in the theft of sensitive data, disruption of business operations, and damage to an organization's reputation. The fact that the Clop ransomware gang is targeting specific software solutions used by various industries highlights the need for organizations to be aware of the potential risks and take proactive measures to secure their systems and data. This includes implementing proper security controls, monitoring systems for suspicious activity, and ensuring that all software and systems are up-to-date and patched.

## 🛠️ Actionable Defense & Mitigation Checklist
1. **Conduct a thorough vulnerability assessment**: Perform a comprehensive vulnerability scan of all internet-exposed Windchill and FlexPLM instances to identify potential vulnerabilities and weaknesses. Use tools such as Nmap, Nessus, or OpenVAS to scan for open ports, services, and potential vulnerabilities.
2. **Implement proper security controls**: Ensure that all Windchill and FlexPLM instances are properly secured with firewalls, intrusion detection and prevention systems, and access controls. Limit access to these systems to only authorized personnel and ensure that all access is logged and monitored.
3. **Keep software up-to-date and patched**: Ensure that all Windchill and FlexPLM software and plugins are up-to-date and patched with the latest security updates. Regularly review and apply security patches and updates to prevent exploitation of known vulnerabilities.
4. **Monitor systems for suspicious activity**: Implement monitoring tools and techniques to detect and respond to suspicious activity on Windchill and FlexPLM systems. Use tools such as SIEM systems, log analysis tools, and anomaly detection tools to identify potential security incidents.
5. **Implement a backup and disaster recovery plan**: Ensure that all Windchill and FlexPLM data is backed up regularly and that a disaster recovery plan is in place in case of a security incident. Regularly test backups and disaster recovery procedures to ensure that they are effective and functional.
