# 🚨 Microsoft to retire the OWA Light client in Exchange Server
**Source**: BleepingComputer
**Link**: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/microsoft/microsoft-announces-owa-light-retirement-in-exchange-server/
**Date**: 2026-07-09

## 📰 Summary of the Threat
The Microsoft announcement to retire the Outlook Web Access (OWA) Light client in Exchange Server marks a significant change in the way users interact with their email services. OWA Light is a lightweight version of the Outlook Web App, designed for older browsers or low-bandwidth connections. However, with the evolution of web technologies and the increasing demand for more feature-rich and secure email experiences, Microsoft has decided to focus on the standard OWA client. This decision targets organizations and users who currently rely on OWA Light, potentially affecting their email accessibility and usability.

The technical mechanism behind this retirement involves Microsoft disabling OWA Light in a future Exchange Server update. This means that users attempting to access their email through OWA Light will be redirected to the standard OWA client or may face compatibility issues if their browsers do not support the newer version. The impact of this change will be most significant for users with outdated browsers or those in areas with low internet connectivity, as they may struggle to adapt to the more resource-intensive standard OWA client.

Organizations should assess their user base and infrastructure to understand the potential impact of this change. They need to evaluate the browsers and devices used by their employees and ensure that these are compatible with the standard OWA client. Additionally, they should consider providing training or support to help users transition smoothly to the new interface and features of the standard OWA client. This proactive approach will minimize disruptions and ensure that email services remain accessible and secure for all users.

## 🛠️ Actionable Defense & Mitigation Checklist
1. **Assess Browser Compatibility**: Conduct an inventory of the browsers used within the organization to ensure they are compatible with the standard OWA client. Focus on browsers that support the latest web standards and security protocols.
2. **Update Exchange Server**: Plan for the upcoming Exchange Server update that will retire OWA Light. Ensure that the update is applied promptly to avoid any compatibility issues or security vulnerabilities that might arise from continued use of OWA Light.
3. **Configure Redirects and Notifications**: Configure redirects from OWA Light to the standard OWA client. Additionally, set up notifications to inform users about the retirement of OWA Light and provide them with instructions on how to access their email using the standard client.
4. **Provide User Training**: Offer training sessions or guides to help users familiarize themselves with the standard OWA client's interface and features. This will reduce the learning curve and minimize support requests related to the transition.
5. **Monitor User Feedback and Issues**: Establish a feedback mechanism to collect user experiences and issues related to the transition. This will help in identifying and addressing any unforeseen problems promptly, ensuring a smooth transition for all users.
