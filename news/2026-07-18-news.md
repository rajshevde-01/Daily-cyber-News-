# 🚨 Abbott probes two cyber incidents amid extortion claims
**Source**: BleepingComputer
**Link**: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/abbott-laboratories-probes-two-cyber-incidents-amid-extortion-claims/
**Date**: 2026-07-18

## 📰 Summary of the Threat
The threat in question involves two separate cybersecurity incidents affecting Abbott Laboratories, a prominent healthcare company. The first incident involves unauthorized access to internal legacy Exact Sciences systems within the company's Cancer Diagnostics business. This breach suggests that attackers may have exploited vulnerabilities in outdated systems or taken advantage of inadequate access controls. The second incident pertains to a claim that attackers breached the company's LabCentral portal, resulting in the theft of company data. This could indicate a phishing campaign, vulnerability exploitation, or insider threat.

The technical mechanism of these vulnerabilities or attacks is not explicitly stated, but common factors could include unpatched software, weak passwords, or social engineering tactics. Given the nature of the targets—internal systems and a portal—attackers may have sought to exploit trust within the network or leverage credentials to move laterally. The fact that extortion claims are involved implies that the attackers may be demanding payment in exchange for not releasing stolen data, adding a layer of complexity to the incident response.

The impact of these incidents could be significant, given the sensitive nature of healthcare data and the potential for disruption to critical services. As such, it's crucial for organizations, especially those in the healthcare sector, to review their security posture, ensure all systems are up-to-date, and implement robust access controls and monitoring to detect and respond to similar threats promptly.

## 🛠️ Actionable Defense & Mitigation Checklist
1. **Conduct a thorough network segmentation review**: Ensure that legacy systems like those mentioned are isolated from the rest of the network to prevent lateral movement in case of a breach. Implement VLANs, firewalls, or other network segmentation tools to restrict access.
2. **Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**: Across all portals and systems, including the LabCentral portal, to add an extra layer of security, making it harder for attackers to gain access using stolen or guessed credentials.
3. **Regularly audit and update software**: Ensure all systems, especially legacy ones, are updated with the latest security patches. Regular audits can help identify outdated software that may be vulnerable to known exploits.
4. **Enhance monitoring and incident response plans**: Invest in tools and training that can help detect anomalies in network traffic and system access patterns, allowing for quicker response times to potential breaches.
5. **Conduct phishing simulations and security awareness training**: Educate employees on how to identify and report phishing attempts, as these are often the entry point for attackers seeking to gain initial access to a network.
