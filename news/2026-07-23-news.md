# 🚨 New msaRAT malware uses Chrome, Edge browsers to route C2 traffic
**Source**: BleepingComputer
**Link**: https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/new-msarat-malware-uses-chrome-edge-browsers-to-route-c2-traffic/
**Date**: 2026-07-23

## 📰 Summary of the Threat
The newly discovered msaRAT malware is a backdoor used by the Chaos ransomware gang to establish command-and-control (C2) communications with compromised systems. This malware leverages a unique evasion technique by routing its C2 traffic through either the Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge browsers installed on the victim's machine. By doing so, it attempts to blend in with legitimate browser traffic, making detection more challenging for security systems. The primary targets of this campaign are not explicitly stated, but given the nature of ransomware attacks, it is likely that the Chaos gang is targeting organizations with valuable data, aiming to extort money in exchange for the decryption key.

The technical mechanism behind msaRAT involves exploiting the trust that security controls place in browser traffic. Since browsers are commonly used and their traffic is often not as heavily scrutinized as other types of network communications, the malware can more easily evade detection. The use of Chrome and Edge specifically may be due to their widespread adoption, making it a strategic choice for maximizing the potential impact of the malware. Understanding the specifics of how msaRAT interacts with these browsers and the network is crucial for developing effective countermeasures.

The implications of msaRAT are significant, as it highlights the evolving nature of cyber threats and the need for continuous adaptation in cybersecurity strategies. Traditional security controls may not be sufficient to detect such sophisticated evasion techniques, emphasizing the importance of advanced threat detection and response capabilities. Furthermore, the involvement of the Chaos ransomware gang suggests a high level of sophistication and potential for significant damage, underscoring the urgency of addressing this threat.

## 🛠️ Actionable Defense & Mitigation Checklist
1. **Implement Browser Traffic Inspection**: Configure security appliances or software to inspect browser traffic more closely. This can involve setting up SSL/TLS inspection to decrypt and analyze traffic that would otherwise appear encrypted and benign.
2. **Enhance Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR)**: Utilize EDR solutions that can monitor endpoint activity for signs of msaRAT or similar malware. This includes looking for unusual browser interactions, unexpected changes in system configuration, or other indicators of compromise (IOCs).
3. **Network Segmentation and Isolation**: Implement strict network segmentation to limit the spread of malware in case of a breach. Isolate systems and networks that do not need to communicate with each other, reducing the potential attack surface.
4. **Regularly Update and Patch Browsers**: Ensure that all browsers, especially Chrome and Edge, are updated with the latest security patches. Outdated browsers can provide an entry point for malware, including msaRAT.
5. **Conduct Regular Security Audits**: Perform periodic security audits to identify vulnerabilities and ensure that all systems and applications are configured securely. This includes reviewing browser extensions, which could potentially be used as a vector for msaRAT or similar threats.
